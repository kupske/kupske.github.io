I am a Full Professor (with tenure) of English, Phonetics and Phonology, and Psycholinguistics at the Universidade Federal do Rio Grande do Sul (UFRGS), where I also serve as faculty in the graduate program PPGLET. I hold an additional position at the PPGLinC at UFBA.

My research addresses the adaptability of native and nonnative speech perception and production across the lifespan, with particular attention to how the bilingual experience itself continuously reshapes language systems. Drawing on Complex Dynamic Systems Theory, Laboratory Phonology, and Embodied Cognition, I investigate how L2 phonological development shapes — and is shaped by — L1 systems, social indexicality, and embodied experience.

I lead the Observatory of Speech (UFRGS) and direct the Speech Sciences Laboratory (LaFALA-UFBA) and the Center for Bilingualism and Complexity Studies. I welcome inquiries from prospective MA, Doctoral, and undergraduate students.

Current Projects

Research

2023 – Present

Embodied Cognition, Body Aesthetics & L2 Phonology

Embodied cognition theory posits that cognitive processes are influenced by sensory and motor experiences, including body aesthetics — how individuals perceive and evaluate their own bodies and those of others. This project investigates the role of embodiment and body aesthetics in L2 speech perception, processing, and development.

Subproject · 2024 – Present

The impact of body aesthetics on the perception of L2 English speech by Brazilian bilinguals

Investigates how visual cues — race, gender identity, and sexual orientation — influence intelligibility and the perceived degree of foreign accent. Employs a mixed-methods approach to understand the cognitive and social mechanisms underlying these effects.

Team: Kupske (UFRGS/CNPq) · Laura Wermuth (PIBIC/CNPq) · Grant: R$ 8.400 · OSF: osf.io/k2dfj
2024 – Present

Speech Perception, Attitudes & Stereotypes

Examines how Brazilian bilingual listeners perceive and judge L2 English spoken with accents from 22 different L1 backgrounds (CSLU Foreign Accented English corpus), and how those perceptions intersect with social stereotypes.

Subproject (a) · 2024 – Present

Accentedness judgment in L2 English spoken by Brazilian bilinguals over the telephone

Focuses on the perception of accentedness in L2 English spoken over the telephone by Brazilian bilinguals at different proficiency levels, using 24 hours of 4,925 utterances from the CSLU FAE corpus.

Kupske, Perozzo (UFRGS) · Wermuth, Fortuna, Bitencourt, Barbosa, da Silva (PIBIC) · OSF: osf.io/bk2ms
Subproject (b) · 2025 – Present

Perceived Accents and Perceived Traits: Attitudes Toward L2 English from Diverse L1 Speakers

Investigates how accent influences social judgments of competence, friendliness, and trustworthiness, and how linguistic variation intersects with social perception, stereotypes, and accent bias.

Kupske · Perozzo (UFRGS)
2023 – Present

Pronunciation Teaching in the Global South

Integrating embodied cognition and decolonial perspectives into L2 speaking and pronunciation teaching practices. This project explores how epistemological repositioning can address linguistic inequalities and promote social justice in language education — drawing on theories developed outside the Global North.

2023 – Present

First Language Attrition & Speech Perception

In light of Complex Dynamic Systems Theory (CDST) and Laboratory Phonology, this project investigates the dynamics of speech perception in L2 English development and — with special attention — in the non-pathological attrition of Brazilian Portuguese as L1 in bilingual contexts. Studies on phonetic-phonological attrition remain rare compared to lexical and syntactic research.

Subproject · 2023 – 2024

Creating perception tasks

Kupske (UFRGS/CNPq) · Bettina Vasata De Oliveira (PIBIC/CNPq) · Grant: R$ 8.400 · Registered: Compesq-Letras, UFRGS

Past projects

2018 – 2024

Bilinguismo e a perda não patológica de língua materna: uma análise da produção e percepção da fala de imigrantes soteropolitanos em Londres e de britânicos em Salvador

Longitudinal investigation of L1 attrition in Brazilian emigrants in London and British immigrants in Salvador, examining both speech production and perception from a CDST perspective.

Grant: CAPES — Chamada Universal · Project No. 432396/2018 · Total: R$ 120.000
2014 – 2017

First Language Attrition: VOT production by Brazilians in London

Investigated Voice Onset Time production in word-initial stop consonants of English and Brazilian Portuguese by Brazilian immigrants residing in London, providing foundational evidence for phonetic L1 attrition under dominant L2 immersion.

Who I am
A Brazilian-Polish linguist. I grew up in a multilingual family somewhere in the pampas.
Based in
Porto Alegre, RS. I've also lived many years in London, Nottingham, and Salvador.
Languages
Portuguese (L1), English, and the occasional attempt at something else. I am currently learning Polish, not to be ashamed of not speaking my family's language.
Outside work
Tea and wine lover. Always reading something about complexity, cognition, or mid-century furniture and interior design. My office looks like a sorcerer's tower: books and tchotchkes collected over a lifetime. Occasional conference escapee who still believes hallway conversations are the best part of academic life. Hopelessly devoted to dogs. Having lunch alone in Covent Garden with Italian food, a glass of wine, and classical music in my ears is, I think, a fairly accurate self-portrait. I believe fireplaces make every room better.
Firm belief
Science should be open, accessible, slow, and written in a way humans can actually read.
